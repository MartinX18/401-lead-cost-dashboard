StoreCost / LeadSearch ISLost to budgetLost to rankRead
Search-network only. This is the share of Search ad impressions you missed because the daily budget ran out (lost to budget) vs. because of ad rank/bid (lost to rank). PMax and Display spend isn’t included, and stores running no search campaigns show “no search data.” A store that’s capped by budget and has at-or-below-average cost per lead is the clearest case to raise the daily budget — you’d likely buy more leads at a similar price. “Lost to rank” means more budget won’t help; that needs bid/quality/landing-page work instead.

Why is cost-per-lead high? · driver split, this period

—
StoreCost / LeadAvg CPCLead conv. rateDiagnosis
Cost per lead = CPC ÷ lead-conversion-rate. Bidding = clicks are pricey (CPC well above group avg) → bid/competition problem. Conversion = clicks come in but the site/offer rarely turns them into a lead (conv rate well below group) → landing-page/offer problem. Broken-tracking stores are skipped (their conv rate isn't real).
